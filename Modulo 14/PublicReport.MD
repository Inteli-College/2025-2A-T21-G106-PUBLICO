## Commission Advance Platform: Real-Estate Broker Liquidity

### Public Report – Module 14 Financial Model

---

### Executive Summary

The **Commission Advance Platform** is a fintech venture that creates a **digital liquidity rail for real-estate brokers and agencies** by advancing sales commissions secured by the **purchase-and-sale agreement** through structured **credit assignment**. Module 14 developed an investor-grade **financial model, projections, DCF, and governance pack** that validate the business’ economic viability under clear risk constraints. The Base 5-year scenario delivers **R$ 16.1M in revenue**, **R$ 4.0M in net profit** (≈ **25% margin**), **R$ 2.1M NPV**, and **~35% ROI**, with positive NPV and profitability preserved even in downside conditions.

---

### Business Model & Value Proposition

- **Target segment**: independent real-estate brokers and small-to-mid agencies with recurring commission flows but limited access to working capital; investors (FIDC and allocators) seeking short-duration, granular exposure to real-estate commission receivables.
- **Product**: **purchase/advance of commission receivables** via credit assignment, backed by the underlying purchase-and-sale contract, with payment routed via agencies or split structures.
- **Revenue model**: monetization through the **interest spread** between the **customer rate (≈5–6% per month, midpoint 5.5%)** and the **funding cost (CDI + 5% a.a.)**, complemented by disciplined discounting and fees; economics are modeled assuming a future **FIDC structure** as capital backbone.
- **Customer value**: fast, compliant liquidity without traditional collateral, transparent pricing, and digital formalization; for investors, a governed, data-rich asset class with concentration limits, tenor caps, and traceable payment flows.

---

### Key Financial Assumptions

- **Pricing & risk**:
  - Customer rate **5–6% per month** (5.5% used as anchor); funding at **CDI + 5% a.a.**, yielding an illustrative monthly spread of **≈4.3%** when CDI = 10% a.a.
  - **Average tenor 30 days** (15–120 days range); **70% advance** on commission as working placeholder.
  - Default / rescission policy anchored at **~0.3% of principal annually** (modeled with conservative monthly provisions and 10% loss at >90 DPD in earlier versions).
- **Volume & growth**:
  - Initial ramp from **~R$20k to ≈R$1.4M** in anticipated monthly volume over 18 months (≈70× growth).
  - Longer-term targets reach **R$0.5M/month by end of Year 1**, **R$2M/month by end of Year 2**, and **R$4M+/month** in the scale phase (Years 3–5).
- **Cost structure**:
  - **Variable costs**: 1% commercial commission on funded amount, messaging/AI, KYC/e-signature, payment split/processor; WhatsApp is effectively **free in Year 1** (non-official provider) with **R$2/message from Year 2** (10% of active base per month).
  - **Fixed OPEX**: progressive ramp of people costs and infrastructure (n8n, Google Workspace, cloud/VPS, Notion, Serasa, e-signature, chatbot), reaching **~R$6.7k–21.8k/month** in early Year 1 and up to **~R$100k–170k/month** by Year 5 as the operation scales.
  - **Taxes**: modeled as **8–15% of gross revenue** pending final Simples Nacional mapping (CNAE/Annex and ISS).

---

### 12–18 Month Outlook & Unit Economics

- **P&L trajectory**:
  - Volume grows from **R$20k to ≈R$1.4M/month** by Period 17; monthly revenue scales from **R$0 to ≈R$49.5k**.
  - Despite strong top-line growth, the model remains **cash-negative over the first 18 months**, with cumulative cash flow of approximately **–R$380k**, reflecting deliberate upfront investment in team, infrastructure, and acquisition.
- **Unit economics**:
  - For each **R$1,000 advanced** (30-day tenor, 5.5% customer rate, CDI 10%), gross spread is **≈R$43.30**; after variable costs (≈R$10–18) and loss provisions, **gross contribution is ≈R$17–25**, implying **40–58% contribution margin** before fixed costs and taxes.
  - **Break-even funded volume** is estimated around **R$2M/month**, where contribution covers OPEX and tax provisions, enabling sustainable EBITDA margins.
- **CAC, LTV & payback**:
  - Year 1 **CAC ≈R$2,000 per new partner**, driven by events, presents, and paid traffic; Year 2 CAC drops to **≈R$1,000** as the brand matures and organic channels grow.
  - With **~3 operations per customer per year** and **≈R$350 revenue per operation**, Year 1 **net LTV ≈R$1,033**, yielding **LTV/CAC ≈0.52× (Year 1)** and **≈1.03× (Year 2)**—below the long-term **≥3.0× target** unless retention and revenue per customer increase.
  - Payback is **~24 months** under Year 1 CAC (≈6 operations) and improves to **≈12 months** under Year 2 CAC, moving into the target **<2-year** range as acquisition efficiency improves.

### 5-Year DCF & Scenario Analysis

- **Base case**:
  - **Revenue ≈R$16.1M**, **Net Profit ≈R$4.0M** (≈**24.7% net margin**).
  - **NPV (1%) ≈R$2.1M**, **IRR ≈4.5%**, **ROI ≈35%**, **ROE ≈4.7×**, with break-even cumulative volume of **≈R$2.9M**.
- **Downside case** (–20% revenue, higher CAC, lower growth, leaner team):
  - Revenue declines to **≈R$12.9M**, Net Profit to **≈R$2.6M** (≈**19.9% margin**).
  - **NPV ≈R$1.2M**, **ROI ≈24%**, **ROE ≈2.8×**—still **positive NPV and attractive returns**, confirming resilience under stress.
- **Optimistic case** (+20% revenue, lower CAC, stronger growth, more people capacity):
  - Revenue rises to **≈R$19.3M**, Net Profit to **≈R$5.4M** (≈**28.1% margin**).
  - **NPV ≈R$3.0M**, **ROI ≈47%**, **ROE ≈6.9×**, signaling **compelling upside** if acquisition efficiency, retention, and volume scaling outperform the base assumptions.
- **Sensitivity focus**:
  - Scenario engine stresses **three core drivers**: **CAC (±10%)**, **people costs (±10%)**, and **growth rate (±20%)**, confirming that growth is the primary value driver, while CAC and headcount discipline are critical to preserving margins and investor returns.

### Governance, Risk & Compliance Framework

- **Credit governance**:
  - Clear role definitions for **Origination**, **Risk/Credit**, **Credit Committee**, **Operations**, and **Compliance/Legal**, with systematic record-keeping (credit files, approvals, periodic reviews).
  - Data-driven onboarding using **Serasa PJ/PF**, operational metrics, and financial indicators to build a structured **Gold/Silver/Bronze** risk matrix.
- **Risk tiers & limits**:
  - **Gold**: healthy partners, **LTV up to 70%**, exposure up to **25% of portfolio** (or a monetary cap), with potential gradual limit increases based on performance.
  - **Silver**: operable with mitigants, **LTV 40–60%**, small initial absolute limits and portfolio share (≈5–10%), mandatory mitigants (short tenors, payment agent/split, additional guarantees).
  - **Bronze**: severe risk profile, **LTV 0%**, no new exposure allowed; existing exposure managed via controlled run-off.
- **Concentration & tenor**:
  - Caps per partner, per final debtor, and by risk band, with strict rules on **maximum exposure** and **tenor**; any breach automatically blocks new operations and escalates to the Credit Committee.
- **Legal robustness of collateral**:
  - Standardized review of purchase-and-sale contracts: formal validity, legality of commission, payment conditions, cancellation (distrato) risk, assignment clauses, and payer consents.
  - Overall **legal status of the underlying** classified as **OK / ALERT / REJECT**, with hard rejects when core legal or assignment conditions are not met.

---

### Key Insights & Next Steps

- **Financial viability**: The model shows **attractive risk-adjusted returns over 5 years**, with positive NPV across scenarios and strong upside potential, but it requires reaching **≈R$2M+/month** in funded volume and gradually optimizing OPEX.
- **Unit economics challenge**: Early **LTV/CAC is below best-practice benchmarks**, reinforcing the need to **drive down CAC**, **increase customer lifetime and operations per partner**, and expand ticket sizes and/or spreads within acceptable risk.
- **Governance as a differentiator**: The **Gold/Silver/Bronze framework**, concentration and tenor policy, and contract-level legal checklist convert opaque commissions into **institutional-grade assets**, increasing investor confidence and supporting a FIDC-based capital structure.
- **Next steps**: refine tax and corporate structure (Simples/CNAE, FIDC setup), calibrate limits and pricing with live data, and use the scenario engine to support **investor discussions, fundraising, and go-to-market sequencing**.
