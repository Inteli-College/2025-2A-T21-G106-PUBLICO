## 2) **Executive summary**

This venture creates a **digital liquidity rail** for real estate brokers and agencies by **advancing sales commissions** secured by the **purchase-and-sale agreement** through structured **credit assignment**. We solve a systemic credit gap—caused by informality, short maturities, regional payment heterogeneity, and rescission risk—by combining a **governed underwriting model**, **API-enabled data collection** from partner agencies, and a **capital structure via FIDC**. Module 16 takes the platform to market: monetizing through a discount rate and service fees on the advanced value, while enforcing concentration, tenor, and flow-of-funds controls to protect investor capital and ensure scale.

---

## 3) **Problematic**

Brazil's real estate value chain locks significant working capital inside commissions that are **hard for banks to underwrite**. Reasons include: (i) limited/fragmented credit history for brokers and small agencies, (ii) **rescission risk** in purchase-and-sale agreements (e.g., title/financing issues), (iii) **short, variable tenors** with **regional payment practices**, (iv) **low ticket averages** with operational idiosyncrasies (e.g., land/rural/incorporation commissions have bigger tickets but higher rescission uncertainty), and (v) **traceability gaps** on receivables flow that raise perceived default risk.
Traditional lenders avoid the segment; as a result, **productive professionals remain illiquid**, unable to fund expenses or invest in growth—even when they hold **financeable receivables**.

## 4) **Description of the enterprise**

We are a **fintech platform** that purchases/advances real estate **commission receivables** via **credit assignment** backed by the underlying **purchase-and-sale contract**.
**What we do:** digitize onboarding, eligibility, and funding for commission advances; **formalize assignment** (terms with the agency and the broker) and **route payment** via the agency or a split mechanism.
**Sector:** Real Estate × Fintech (Receivables Finance).
**Solution:** governed underwriting + API integrations + electronic formalization; investor visibility via dashboards.
**Business model:** discount rate on advanced value + service fees; FIDC acquires eligible receivables.
**Benchmark references:** RLTY Capital (US), PipeImob and Imobitech (BR). Our focus is **commission advance as the core product**, not as a side feature of a pay-out system.

## 7) **Scope**

### a) **Preliminary Value Proposition**

**For brokers:** immediate, compliant liquidity on earned commissions—**no waiting 30 to 90 days**, no traditional collateral, transparent pricing, and **fully digital** formalization with clear liability boundaries protecting the broker from recourse exposure.
**For agencies:** a differentiated benefit that strengthens broker loyalty and retention, supported by a governed payment split mechanism that keeps the agency within the transaction flow without operational burden.
**For key accounts (large agencies and franchise networks):** a structured, scalable product with documented underwriting criteria and an institutional-grade capital structure—making the product credible for high-volume distribution.

### b) **Customer Segments**

* **Primary:** independent real estate brokers and small-to-mid agencies in São Paulo with recurring sales activity and limited access to working capital.
* **Secondary:** regional franchise networks and large independent agencies in the Southeast and Midwest acting as distribution multipliers.
* **Influence layer:** former account managers and senior professionals from leading proptech platforms (e.g., QuintoAndar, Loft) who hold trusted relationships with agency decision-makers and franchise operators.

**Priority sub-segments & nuances:**

* **São Paulo urban brokers** operating in high-volume, bank-financed transactions—highest frequency and predictable commission cycles, lower rescission risk.
* **Franchise network members (e.g., Re/Max, Century 21)** where standardized contracts reduce documentation friction and accelerate onboarding.
* **Award-recognized agencies** targeted through event sponsorships, as they signal operational maturity and creditworthiness.

## 8) **General observations**

**Premises:** operational platform ready for customer intake; capital structure (FIDC or equivalent facility) in place to fund advances; signed agency partnership agreements enabling payment split or escrow routing; ambassador network contracted and briefed; event participation confirmed for at least two anchor channels in Sprint 2.
**Restrictions:** geographic focus on the São Paulo metro in Year 1; disciplined CAC ceilings for standard and key accounts; no direct broker payments (all disbursements routed via agency or verified split mechanism); eligible-ticket envelope and concentration caps per client/agency.
**Key risks & mitigations:** product distrust among brokers (mitigated by ambassador credibility, educational materials, transparent documentation, and liability-boundary clarity); agency resistance to payment flow participation (mitigated by standardized split agreements, minimal operational burden, and value proposition framing around broker retention); broker liability fear (mitigated by explicit assignment terms that isolate the broker from recourse risk and by onboarding materials that address this point directly).
**Hypotheses to validate:** ambassador network conversion efficiency vs. direct channel; event-sourced CAC vs. target thresholds; agency willingness to recommend product proactively once enrolled; onboarding material effectiveness in reducing time-to-first-advance; pricing sensitivity relative to perceived urgency of liquidity need.
**Strategic insight:** by positioning launch through **trusted human intermediaries** (ambassador network) rather than cold digital acquisition, the platform lowers the trust barrier inherent to a new financial product in a relationship-driven market—converting industry credibility into a structured, measurable acquisition engine.
